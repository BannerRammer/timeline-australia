Women's Suffrage

Before Federation, only women around South Australia and Western Australia had women's rights however the other states wanted to have women's rights. So after after the Parliament was made, women protested for women's rights to vote and in 1902, an act had passed just so that women over the age of 21 can vote. This was a first in the world. However, women over the age of 21 were allowed to vote federal elections, not local or state elections. So during the 1890s and 1920s, women protested and the states had allowed women to vote state and local elections as well but at different times.

Gallipoli Landings, April 15th, 1915

On April 15th, 1915, Australian and New Zealand forces (aka ANZACS) attacked Gallipoli, a beach in modern day Turkey to reach the capital, Constantinople to get rid of the Ottomon Empire from joining the war. However, they landed on the wrong beach and the Ottomons won the battle in late 1915 and early 1916. More than 100000 ANZACS lost their lives.

Fastest Woman in Australia, 2000

Cathy Freeman, a track and field athlete won gold at the 2000 Sydney Olympics, she was the second Aboriginal Woman to win gold at the olympics and one of the fastest woman in the world. Ranked at 6th place. She became a role model to all Australians and made her own charity called Cathy Freeman Foundation where they help young Aboriginal kids experiencing abuse, racism, and poverty.

The First Woman Prime Minister of Australia

Julia Gillard, the 27th Prime Minister of Australia made history as she became the first woman prime minister of Australia on the 24th of June, 2010. She swore into office by the first Woman Governer-General Quentin Bryce.
