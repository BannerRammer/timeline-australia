The First Gold, 1851

After seeing how similar Australia and California land are, the Edward Hargreeves went back to Orange, Victoria and found that there's gold in Australia. After this discovery, many free settlers from the US, Germany, Ireland, Britain, New Zealand and Scotland came to Australia in search of gold. This sparked a Gold Rush to occur around the 1850s.

The First Gold Mine, May 1851

After the discovery in gold in Orange, Victoria, convicts and free settlers came to Orange and founded the first gold mine in the gold rush. It was estimated that over 300 miners and diggers were at the site in May 1851.

The Victorian Gold Mines, 1851

More and more gold mines were popping up all around Victoria, around five gold mines had a major significants on what shaped Australia today. These gold mines are, the Ballarat gold fields, Bendigo mine, Orange mine, and Castlemaine.

The New South Wales Mines, June 1851

After the Castlemaine mine was founded, gold was found in Sofala, New South Wales and after this discovery, in the late September and December of 1851, more and more gold fields popped up in New South Wales, these places are, Araluen, Braidwood, Bathurst, Bell River, and Tuena.

Tasmanian Gold Fields, 1851 - 1886

In 1857, in Tasmania, gold was found and around the 1850s and 1880s, a gold rush occured and more than 5 gold fields were found. One major gold field was in Managa.

South Australia, 1852

In Mary 1852, Payable gold was found in Echunga in Adelaide Hills, South Australia. The Echunga gold field was founded by William Chapman, Thomas Hardiman and Henry Hampton. There were all payed by the South Australian government $1000 to setup a gold field there.

Queensland mines, 1867 - 1870

Years after Queensland seperated from New South Wales, The Queensland government set a reward of $3000 to find gold around Queensland. Gold were found in Queensland however, the most major gold field found was in Gympie found by James Nash.

1870s - 1900s

More minor mines were found all around Australia, including ones in the Norther Territory and Western Australia. The last mine ever closed is the mine at Pine Creek in the Northern Territory.

The Eureka Stockade

Due to miners paying around 30 shillings for permits to mine. The miners got mad about that since most of the miners were dirt poor. So in 1854, Miners got together and made a Eureka flag. They put it in the middle of them and burned their permits. Then on the evening, a battle broke out between the miners and the police/army. More than 20 miners died and around 6 police men and soldiers died in the battle. The people that started the Eureka Stockade were charged however weren't put to jail.

The Impact of the Gold Rush

The gold rush made new towns, and also made Australia diverse bringing in foreign people into Australia and bringing their religion and culture to Australia.
