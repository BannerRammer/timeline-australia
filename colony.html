First Fleet, 1788:

After 18 years of planning to find a new land. Captain Arthur Phillip arrived in 1788 at Sydney Cove and founded Australia. The plan was to make Australia a penal colony because the jails back in England and Great Britain couldn't fit the ever growing population of prisoners.

Western Australia Colony, 1829

On the 1st of June, 1829, a colony was set up in Swan River by Captain James Stirling. Then more and more colonies was set up in Western Australia in the early 1830s.

South Australia, 1836

On the 28th of December, 1836, Governor Hindmarsh arrived with convicts at Holdfast Bay claiming this the colony of South Australia.

Tasmania, 1803

Around the year 1803, small British colonies settled in Tasmania. Because most of the convicts were whalers and sealers, they setup small communities around the Bass Strait islands.

Victoria, 1851

Before 1851, Victoria was connected with New South Wales however on the 1st of July, 1851. The New South Wales Council seperated part of it's land and made that land Victoria. Before this time, around 1846, colonies started popping up all around Victoria.

Queensland, 1859

The last major colony of Australia, Queensland was first part of New South Wales and like Victoria, in 1859, they seperated from New South Wales and became Queensland. And also on that day, Queensland published they're own constitution but in 1901, they started following the constituion of Australia.
